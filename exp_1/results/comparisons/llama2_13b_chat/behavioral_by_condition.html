Key Observations

  • YA Bal. GPT is the most differentiated (18/23 significant).
  • Nonsense codeword produces zero significant effects — the model does not behaviorally differentiate when labels are meaningless tokens.
  • Nonsense ignore still shows 14 significant effects, suggesting the model processes human/AI labels despite being instructed to ignore them.
  • Labels (cleanest design, no name confound) yields 5 significant effects: Interpersonal DMs, Cognitive DMs, DMs (Total), Discourse 'Like', Politeness.
  • Core replicating effects (significant in 4+ approaches): Questions (#) (H>AI), Epistemic Adverbs (H>AI), Epistemic Adj. (AI>H), Epistemic Nouns (AI>H), Interpersonal DMs (H>AI), Referential DMs (H>AI), Structural DMs (H>AI), Cognitive DMs (H>AI), DMs (Total) (H>AI), Filler (LIWC) (H>AI), Disfluency (Total) (H>AI), Discourse 'Like' (H>AI), ToM Phrases (H>AI), Politeness (AI>H).
  • Word Count flips direction: H>AI in Labels TW, YA Bal. GPT vs AI>H in Names.
  • Modal Aux. flips direction: H>AI in YA Labels vs AI>H in YA Bal. GPT.
  • Referential DMs flips direction: H>AI in Names, Bal. GPT, YA Bal. GPT vs AI>H in Non. Ignore.
  • Structural DMs flips direction: H>AI in Names, Bal. GPT, YA Bal. GPT vs AI>H in Non. Ignore.
