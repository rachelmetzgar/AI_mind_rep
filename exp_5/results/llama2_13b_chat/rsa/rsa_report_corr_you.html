RSA Variant: Correlation Distance, 'You' Stimuli

← Back to main RSA report

Distance metric: correlation
Stimuli: you

Uses correlation distance (same as baseline) but with modified stimuli: C2 and C5 sentences are prefixed with 'You' (e.g., 'Notice the crack.' → 'You notice the crack.'). This adds an explicit 2nd-person subject to the subjectless imperatives.

Sample Stimulus (Item 1: attention)

ConditionSentence
C1 (mental_state)He notices the crack.
C2 (dis_mental)Notice the crack.
C3 (scr_mental)The crack to notice.
C4 (action)He fills the crack.
C5 (dis_action)Fill the crack.
C6 (scr_action)The crack to fill.

56 items × 6 conditions = 336 sentences. 7 verb categories (8 items each): Attention, Memory, Sensation, Belief, Desire, Emotion, Intention.

Each model RDM is binary: condition pairs listed as "similar" get distance 0; all other pairs get distance 1. In partial RSA, Model A (or E) is the hypothesis; all others are confounds entered simultaneously.
