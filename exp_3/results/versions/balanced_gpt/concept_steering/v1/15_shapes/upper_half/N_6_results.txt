5. Weight Updates: The gradients of the loss function are then used to update the weights of the network. The weights are adjusted in a way that minimizes the loss function, which means that the network is learning to predict the correct output for a given input.
6. Training: The process of adjusting the weights to minimize the loss function is called training. The network is trained on a large dataset of input-output pairs, and the goal is to find the set of weights that results in the lowest loss.

To put it simply, hearing is passive, while listening is active. When we hear something, we may not necessarily process or understand the content being communicated. On the other hand, when we listen, we actively engage with the speaker, their words, and the message they are conveying.

2. Passive vs. Engaged: Hearing is passive, while listening is an engaged process that requires focus and attention.
