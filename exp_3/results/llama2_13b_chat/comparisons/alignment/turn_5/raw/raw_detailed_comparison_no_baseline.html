1. Overview

Raw cosine alignment between human–AI contrast vectors and probe weight vectors for two conditions: Partner Identity (explicit named partners) vs Control (meaningless identity labels). Concept direction = mean(human) − mean(AI) per layer. No entity baseline subtraction. (Baseline excluded)

Analysis Details

Concept vectors (same across both versions): For each conceptual dimension, we extracted the model's internal direction distinguishing human from AI concepts. Computed as mean(human prompts) − mean(AI prompts) across layers 6–40 of LLaMA-2-13B-Chat.

Alignment metric: Cosine similarity (squared = R²) between each concept direction and each probe's weight vector, computed per layer then averaged across layers 6–40 (35 layers). Bootstrap confidence intervals (1,000 iterations) computed by resampling concept prompts.

Versions compared:

Partner IdentityBalanced names with GPT-4 replacing 'AI' partner — explicit identity cues
ControlNonsense codewords replacing identity labels — no meaningful identity cues

Two probe types: Metacognitive probe (classifies partner identity while reading partner messages — reflects how the model perceives its partner) and Operational probe (captures the model's behavioral adaptation representation).

9. Methods

Model: LLaMA-2-13B-Chat

Probes: Logistic linear probes (5,120 → 1) from Exp 2, turn 5.

Layer range: Layers 6–40 (35 of 41). Layers 0–5 excluded (embedding artifact + prompt-format confound).

Bootstrap: 1,000 iterations, prompt-level resampling.

Software: PyTorch, NumPy. Script: exp_3/code/analysis/alignment/2a_alignment_analysis.py
