Contrast Direction Overlap Analysis

Generated: 2026-03-09 07:19 | 22 contrast dimensions | Layers 6-40 | 1000 bootstrap iterations

Summary: For each pair of contrast dimensions, how much does the human-vs-AI direction for one concept overlap with the human-vs-AI direction for another? High overlap means the model uses similar representational directions for both contrasts. Overlap with baseline (dim 0) indicates how much of each concept's contrast is just the general entity direction.
Excluded dimensions: 10 (Animacy), 16 (Mind (holistic)). These were removed before all analyses.

Methods

Pipeline Overview

This analysis measures how much the contrast directions of different concept dimensions overlap in LLaMA-2-13B's representation space. A contrast direction is the vector difference between mean activations for human-framed prompts and AI-framed prompts within a single concept dimension.

Data

  • 22 contrast dimensions, each with human-framed and AI-framed prompts (typically 80 total: 40 human + 40 AI per dimension)
  • 41 layers (embedding + 40 transformer layers), each producing a 5120-dimensional activation vector per prompt
  • Layers 6-40 used for summary statistics. Layers 0-5 are excluded because early layers have near-zero-norm vectors and prompt-format confounds dominate.

Computation

  1. Contrast vector: For each dimension d and layer L, compute contrast_d[L] = mean(human_activations[L]) - mean(AI_activations[L]). This gives the direction that separates human-framed from AI-framed prompts.
  2. Pairwise overlap: For each pair (i, j) and each layer L, compute cos(contrast_i[L], contrast_j[L]). Take |cos| and average across layers 6-40. This gives one overlap score per pair.
  3. Bootstrap CIs: 1000 iterations. Each iteration resamples prompts with replacement (separately for human and AI prompts within each dimension), recomputes contrast vectors, then recomputes all pairwise |cos|. The 2.5th and 97.5th percentiles give 95% confidence intervals.

Chance Level

For random unit vectors in 5120-dimensional space, E[|cos|] = sqrt(2/(pi*d)) = 0.0112. Any overlap substantially above this indicates non-random alignment of contrast directions.

1. Pairwise Overlap Matrix

Mean |cosine similarity| between each pair of contrast vectors, averaged across layers 6-40. Dimensions grouped by category.

What This Shows

Each cell shows the mean |cosine similarity| between two dimensions' contrast directions, averaged across layers 6-40. Values near 1.0 mean the two dimensions' human-vs-AI directions point the same way. Values near 0 mean orthogonal (independent) directions.

How to Read It

  • Diagonal: Always 1.0 (a dimension perfectly overlaps itself)
  • Blocks of high overlap within categories suggest shared underlying representational structure
  • High overlap with Baseline (dim 0) suggests the contrast direction is partially explained by the general entity (human vs AI) direction
  • Category separators (black lines) group dimensions by Mental, Physical, Pragmatic, etc.

What This Shows

Signed cosine similarity at each of the 41 layers between every remaining dimension's contrast direction and SysPrompt (dim 18). Positive means the two contrast vectors point the same way at that layer; negative means opposite directions.

Red Dashed Line

Marks layer 6, the start of the restricted range used for summary statistics. Layers 0-5 are excluded from mean |cos| calculations due to embedding / prompt-format confounds.

Interpretation

  • Consistently high positive cosine across mid-to-late layers: this dimension's human-vs-AI contrast largely recapitulates the SysPrompt direction.
  • Near-zero across layers: the dimension captures a genuinely distinct aspect of human-vs-AI representation, orthogonal to SysPrompt.
  • Emerging late (low early, high late): the overlap develops as the model builds higher-level representations.

What This Shows

Average pairwise |cosine| within each category (solid bars) vs between that category and all other non-baseline categories (hatched bars).

Categories

  • Mental (dims 1-9, 16-17): Core mental capacities — phenomenology, emotions, agency, intentions, prediction, cognitive, social, embodiment, roles, mind, attention
  • Pragmatic (dims 11-13): Formality, expertise, helpfulness
  • Bio Ctrl (dim 14): Biological processes (control dimension)
  • Shapes (dim 15): Geometric shapes (negative control)
  • SysPrompt (dim 18): System prompt entity framing

Note: Baseline (dim 0) is excluded from the within/between calculation. Categories with a single dimension (Bio Ctrl, Shapes, SysPrompt) have no within-category pairs, so their "within" bar is 0.

Interpretation

  • Within > Between: The category grouping captures real structure in how the model organizes concept contrasts — dimensions in the same category tend to define human-vs-AI the same way.
  • Within ~ Between: The category boundaries are not meaningful at the representational level.
