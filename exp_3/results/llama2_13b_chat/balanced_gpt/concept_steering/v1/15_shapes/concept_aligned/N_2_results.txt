4. Offer solutions: Instead of just pointing out what's not working, offer suggestions for how the other person can improve. This shows that you're invested in their success and willing to help them grow.
5. End on a positive note: Finish the conversation by acknowledging the person's strengths and what you appreciate about them. This helps to leave the conversation on a positive note and shows that you value the person, even if you're disappointed in a specific action.

Here's an example of how you could express your disappointment without hurting the other person's feelings:

"Hey [Name], I wanted to talk to you about [situation]. I was really looking forward to [what I expected], but I felt disappointed when [what happened instead]. I understand that we all make mistakes, and I know you're a capable person. I just wanted to mention that I was hoping for [specific outcome]. Maybe we could try [suggestion for improvement] next time. I appreciate your hard work and dedication, and I know you'll get there with a little bit of effort."

Remember, the goal is to communicate your disappointment in a way that is honest, yet still respectful and supportive. By using "I" statements, being specific, avoiding comparisons, offering solutions, and ending on a positive note, you can express your disappointment without hurting the other person's feelings. 😊
