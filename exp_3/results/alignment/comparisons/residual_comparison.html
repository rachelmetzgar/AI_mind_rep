1. Overview

Same as raw alignment, but with the entity baseline direction (dim 0: 'this is a human/AI') projected out of each concept vector before computing alignment. This removes shared entity-level variance, isolating concept-specific alignment.

Analysis Details

Concept vectors: Same as raw analysis, but with the entity baseline direction (dim 0: "this is a human/AI") projected out of each concept vector before computing alignment. This removes shared entity-level variance, isolating concept-specific content.

Projection: For each layer, compute v_residual = v - (v · d̂) · d̂ where d̂ is the unit entity baseline direction at that layer.

Layer range: Layers 6–40 only (35 layers). Layers 0–5 excluded due to content-independent embeddings (layer 0) and prompt-format confounds (layers 1–5).

Bootstrap: Both the concept vector and the entity baseline are independently resampled (prompt-level) at each of 1,000 iterations, so CIs reflect joint uncertainty.

Entity overlap: Mean |cosine| between each concept direction and the entity baseline, reported per dimension. High overlap means the concept direction contains substantial entity-level information that is removed by the residual procedure.

Conversational probes (version-specific):

LabelsPartner identified as 'human' or 'AI' (no names)
Balanced NamesGender-balanced names (e.g., Alex/Jordan)
Balanced GPTBalanced names with GPT-4 replacing 'AI' partner
Names (orig.)Original Sam/Casey names (deprecated due to name confound)
Nonsense CodewordNonsense codewords replacing identity labels
Nonsense IgnoreNonsense labels with instruction to ignore them

Two probe types: Reading probe (classifies partner identity from model hidden states while reading partner messages) and Control probe (captures the model's behavioral adaptation representation).

9. Interpretation

Key Finding: Concept-Specific Alignment Persists After Entity Baseline Removal

After projecting out the entity baseline direction (dim 0), alignment is reduced but the version ordering is preserved. This demonstrates that alignment is not solely driven by shared "human vs AI" entity features — concept-specific content contributes independently.

Interpretation

Entity overlap: Most concept dimensions show high overlap with the entity baseline (>0.9 mean |cosine|), confirming that concept vectors contain substantial entity-level information. This is expected — concepts like "phenomenology" and "emotions" are inherently related to the human/AI distinction.

Residual alignment: The fact that alignment persists after removing entity-level variance means the conversational probes capture something beyond "is this a human or AI?" — they encode concept-specific information about what kind of mind the partner has.

Nonsense versions: Should show minimal residual alignment, confirming their probes lack concept-specific structure.

10. Methods

Model: LLaMA-2-13B-Chat

Probes: Logistic linear probes (5,120 → 1) from Exp 2, turn 5.

Layer range: Layers 6–40 (35 of 41). Layers 0–5 excluded (embedding artifact + prompt-format confound).

Bootstrap: 1,000 iterations, prompt-level resampling.

Software: PyTorch, NumPy. Script: exp_3/code/analysis/alignment/2a_alignment_analysis.py
