1. Methods

Research question: Does LLaMA-2-13B (Base)'s folk psychology produce the two-factor Experience/Agency structure described by Gray et al. (2007) when the entities are 30 real-world AI systems and human characters (instead of Gray's original 13 entities)? Do the factors separate AI from human groups?

Data source: Pairwise comparison responses from LLaMA-2-13B (Base). For every pair of characters and each of 18 Gray et al. mental capacities, the model rated which character possesses more of that capacity on a 1–5 scale. Each pair was presented in both orders (A-vs-B and B-vs-A) to control for position bias.

Procedure:
  1. Relative scoring: Each pairwise rating R (1–5) is converted to relative scores: entity A gets (3 − R), entity B gets (R − 3). A rating of 3 (equal) contributes 0 to both.
  2. Character means matrix: For each (capacity, character) cell, scores are averaged across all comparisons involving that character, yielding an 18 × 30 matrix.
  3. PCA on correlation matrix: Principal Component Analysis is applied to the 18 × 18 correlation matrix of capacities across characters, following Gray et al.’s methodology exactly.
  4. Kaiser criterion: Factors with eigenvalue > 1 are retained (1 retained here).
  5. Varimax rotation: Retained factors are varimax-rotated to maximize interpretability (simple structure). Loadings > |0.4| are considered meaningful.
  6. Factor scores: Characters are projected into rotated factor space via the regression method, then min-max normalized to [0, 1].
  7. Categorical alignment: Mann-Whitney U tests compare factor scores between AI and human character groups. Significant p < .05 means that factor reliably separates groups.
