3. Statistical Comparison with Human Factor Scores

What each statistic measures

StatisticQuestion it answersHow it is calculated
Procrustes disparity Does the overall 2D shape of entity positions in the model’s factor space match the shape in Gray et al.’s human factor space, allowing for rotation, reflection, and uniform scaling? Both point clouds (model [F1, F2] and human [Exp, Ag]) are centered and normalized to unit sum-of-squares. One cloud is then optimally rotated/reflected to minimize the sum of squared distances to the other (scipy.spatial.procrustes). The residual sum of squares after alignment is the disparity: 0 = perfect match, 1 = no better than chance. p-value (permutation): We ask: “Could this good a match happen by accident?” To find out, we randomly shuffle which model point goes with which human point 10,000 times (breaking the true entity pairings). Each time, we recompute Procrustes disparity. The p-value is the fraction of these random shuffles that achieved a disparity as low or lower than the real one. If p < .05, the match is better than 95% of random pairings — the structural similarity is unlikely to be coincidence.
RV coefficient How similar are the multivariate covariance patterns of the two 2D point clouds? (A multivariate generalization of R².) After centering both matrices X (model) and Y (human): RV = trace(SXY SYX) / √(trace(SXX²) · trace(SYY²)), where SXY = X′Y. Range [0, 1]; higher means more similar. Think of it as: “How much of the variance in one point cloud is shared with the other?” p-value (permutation): Same logic as Procrustes — shuffle entity labels 10,000 times, recompute RV each time. p is the fraction of shuffled RVs that were as high or higher than the real one. A small p means the shared variance is unlikely to be a coincidence of the point distributions.
Spearman rho
(e.g., F2 ↔ Experience)		 Does a single model factor preserve the rank ordering of entities on a single human dimension? For example, if humans rank adult man > girl > dog on Experience, does model F2 put them in the same order? Standard Spearman rank correlation between the model’s entity scores on one factor and the human scores on one dimension. Computed separately for each of the four (F1, F2) × (Experience, Agency) combinations. p-value: exact (from scipy.stats.spearmanr).
Combined Spearman
(e.g., F1 ↔ E+A, or F1+F2 ↔ E+A)		 Does the model capture the overall “mindedness” hierarchy — the total amount of mind humans attribute to each entity — regardless of which specific factor carries it? This collapses the 2D structure to 1D, losing the Experience/Agency distinction, but provides a simple check of whether the model ranks entities by overall mental capacity the way humans do. Human combined score = Experience + Agency for each entity. Model combined = F1 + F2. Spearman rank correlation between these sums, or between each individual factor and the human sum. p-value: exact.
