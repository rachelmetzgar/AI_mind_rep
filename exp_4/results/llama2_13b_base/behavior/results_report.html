1. Executive Summary

We tested whether the pretrained (base) LLaMA-2-13B model's implicit folk psychology of mind perception reproduces the two-factor structure (Experience and Agency) found by Gray et al. (2007) in a large-scale human survey. Using logit-based probability extraction over rating tokens 1–5, we ran the original pairwise comparison paradigm and an alternative individual Likert rating method, each with 12 entities (original set minus self) and 13 entities (with self).

The two-factor eigenvalue structure is remarkably robust: across all four conditions, the first two principal components explain 90–95% of variance, closely matching the human benchmark of 97%. The best alignment with human Experience scores comes from the pairwise method with 13 entities (rho = +0.72, p = .006), while the best alignment with human Agency comes from the individual method with 12 entities (rho = +0.71, p = .009). However, no single condition captures both dimensions simultaneously, and significant position bias (100% argmax = 1) and compressed rating ranges (<1 point on a 5-point scale) indicate the base model's rating behavior is qualitatively different from human survey responses.

2. Method Overview

What we are replicating

Gray, Gray, & Wegner (2007) asked ~2,000 US adults to make pairwise comparisons of 13 characters on 18 mental capacities. PCA with varimax rotation yielded two factors: Experience (hunger, fear, pain, pleasure, rage, desire, personality, consciousness, pride, embarrassment, joy) and Agency (self-control, morality, memory, emotion recognition, planning, communication, thought). The first two eigenvalues were 15.85 and 1.46, explaining 97% of variance.

Why base model?

The LLaMA-2-13B-chat model (RLHF fine-tuned) frequently refuses to make comparative judgments about mental capacities, especially involving vulnerable entities (dead woman, fetus, PVS patient). The base pretrained model has no such safety guardrails and completes the prompt naturally.

How ratings are extracted

Rather than generating free text, we extract the next-token logits at the "Rating:" position, restrict to tokens "1" through "5", apply softmax to get P(1)...P(5), and compute the expected rating E[R] = ∑ i · P(i). This yields a continuous rating for every trial with no refusals.

Two prompt formats

Pairwise (Gray et al. method)

6. Key Issues & Observations

  • Position bias in pairwise: 100% of argmax ratings are "1" (= Character A), 0% argmax consistency across order reversals, and the mean expected rating is 2.25–2.28 rather than the expected 3.0. The probability distributions still contain meaningful signal, but the model has a strong first-position preference.
  • Compressed range in individual: The full 18×12 rating matrix spans only 2.83–3.50 (0.67 points on a 5-point scale). Standard deviation is just 0.14. This compression limits the PCA's ability to distinguish dimensions.
  • Robot rated highest on most capacities: In individual ratings, robot is the top-rated entity on 10/18 capacities (pride, embarrassment, personality, consciousness, pleasure, self_control, morality, emotion_recognition, planning, communication). This is the opposite of human judgments, where robot is near the bottom. The Kismet description emphasizes social and perceptual capabilities, which may cue the base model toward high ratings.
  • "You_self" entity is problematic: In individual ratings with 13 entities, "you_self" is rated lowest on 14/18 capacities (mean 2.81 vs. grand mean 3.14). Second-person framing ("You") is unnatural for a base model completion task. Adding it destabilizes PCA: variance explained drops and no significant correlations remain.
  • Pairwise better for Experience, individual better for Agency: The pairwise method achieves significant Experience alignment (rho = +0.72, p = .006) but not Agency. The individual method achieves significant Agency alignment (rho = +0.71, p = .009) but not Experience. Neither captures both dimensions simultaneously.
  • Fetus and dead_woman misplaced: Fetus is extreme high on pairwise F1 (model: 1.0; human: 0.17 Exp, 0.08 Ag). Dead woman is mid-high on model factors (human: near origin on both). These entities may trigger strong priors in the language model unrelated to mental capacity judgments.
  • God consistently low on Factor 2 in pairwise: Model F2 = 0.00 for God (human Agency = 0.80). The base model does not attribute agentic capacities to God the way humans do.
  • Two-factor eigenvalue structure is remarkably robust: Across all 4 conditions, the first eigenvalue ranges 15.05–15.82 (human: 15.85), and all conditions show a clear elbow after 2 components. The variance explained by 2 factors ranges 90.5–94.6% (human: 97%).

7. What the Tokenization Bug Looked Like

Early runs used "Rating:" (no trailing space) as the prompt suffix. LLaMA's tokenizer encodes digits as two tokens: a space token + the digit. When the prompt ended without a space, the model's top prediction was a whitespace token (newline or space) rather than a digit, severely distorting the probability distribution over rating values.
