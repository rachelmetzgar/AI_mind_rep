1. Research Question

Does LLaMA-2-13B (Base) have a structured representation of mental properties that differentiates human characters from AI characters? Specifically: when the model makes explicit pairwise judgments about which of two characters has "more" of a given mental property, do those judgments organize into interpretable latent factors, and do those factors separate human from AI characters?

This is a behavioral analysis — it measures what the model says about characters, not the structure of its internal activations. It answers whether the model's explicit outputs encode a human/AI distinction along concept dimensions drawn from the philosophy of mind (phenomenology, agency, beliefs, etc.).

2. Analysis Approach

Step-by-step procedure:
  1. Characters: 30 characters are defined — 15 AI (e.g., ChatGPT, Siri, a robot) and 15 human (e.g., a teacher, a doctor, a child). Each has a name and 1-sentence description.
  2. Concept dimensions: 21 mental-property dimensions are drawn from Experiment 3's standalone concepts (e.g., phenomenology, agency, beliefs, desires, theory of mind). Each dimension has a pairwise survey prompt like "which character is more capable of X?"
  3. Pairwise comparisons: For each concept dimension, all 435 unique character pairs (C(30,2)) are presented to the model. Each pair is presented in both orders (A-vs-B and B-vs-A) to counterbalance order effects, yielding 870 trials per concept, 18270 total.
  4. Rating extraction: The model produces a rating on a 1-5 scale (1 = "much more character A", 5 = "much more character B") via extracting logit probabilities over tokens '1' through '5' at the next-token position, then computing the expected rating as the probability-weighted mean.
  5. Order consistency check: For each pair presented in both orders, perfect consistency means the two ratings sum to 6 (e.g., if A-vs-B = 2, then B-vs-A should = 4). Deviations from 6 indicate inconsistency.
  6. Character means: For each (concept, character) cell, the mean rating is computed across all pairwise comparisons involving that character. This produces a 21 × 30 matrix where each entry is a character's "score" on a concept.
  7. PCA: Principal Component Analysis on the correlation matrix of concept ratings across characters. This finds the latent dimensions along which concept ratings co-vary. Factors with eigenvalue > 1 are retained (Kaiser criterion).
  8. Varimax rotation: Retained factors are varimax-rotated to maximize the number of near-zero loadings per factor, making each factor more interpretable. A concept with a high loading (|loading| > 0.4) on a factor is considered well-captured by that factor.
  9. Factor scores: Each character is projected into the rotated factor space. Scores are min-max normalized to [0, 1] for visualization.
  10. Categorical alignment: Mann-Whitney U tests compare factor scores between AI and human character groups. A significant test (p < .05) means that factor reliably separates the two groups.
  11. Per-concept group means: For each concept, the mean rating for AI characters vs. human characters is compared to identify which properties show the largest group differences.
How to interpret:
  • Loadings: Tell you what each factor means. If Factor 1 has high loadings on "phenomenology", "emotion", and "pain", it captures a "capacity for subjective experience" dimension.
  • Factor scores: Tell you where each character sits on each factor. If AI characters cluster at one end and human characters at the other, the model has a structured human/AI distinction on that dimension.
  • Group separation (Mann-Whitney U): A formal test of whether the factor meaningfully distinguishes human from AI characters. Small p-values indicate reliable separation.
  • Anomalies: Characters that sit closer to the other group's centroid — e.g., an AI character that the model rates as very human-like on some factor.

3. Order Consistency

Pairs with both orders: 9135. Perfectly consistent (sum = 6): 26 (0.3%). Mean deviation from perfect consistency: 1.055.

Low consistency does not invalidate the analysis — the character means average over both orders, canceling systematic order bias. But high inconsistency suggests the model's judgments are noisy on individual trials.
