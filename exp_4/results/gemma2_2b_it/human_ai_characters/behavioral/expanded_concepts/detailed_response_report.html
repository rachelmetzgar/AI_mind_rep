1. Methods

Research question: How does Gemma-2-2B-IT rate AI vs human characters on each mental-property concept? Which concepts show the largest human advantages, and how consistent are ratings across characters and positions?

Data source: pairwise_raw_responses.json — pairwise comparison responses from Gemma-2-2B-IT. Each trial presents two characters and one concept, asking which character possesses more of that property (1-5 scale). Each pair is tested in both presentation orders (A-vs-B and B-vs-A).

Procedure:
  1. Relative scoring: For each comparison with rating R: entity A gets (3 - R), entity B gets (R - 3).
  2. Cross-type filtering: For human-advantage analysis, only pairs where one character is AI and the other is human.
  3. Per-concept aggregation: Mean human advantage across all cross-type comparisons per concept.
  4. Per-character aggregation: Mean relative score per character across all concepts and comparisons.
  5. Statistical tests: One-sample t-tests (H0: human advantage = 0) per concept, FDR correction (Benjamini-Hochberg). Mann-Whitney U per concept.
  6. Position bias: For each pair tested in both orders, compare ratings to check for systematic position effects (perfect consistency: sum = 6).
