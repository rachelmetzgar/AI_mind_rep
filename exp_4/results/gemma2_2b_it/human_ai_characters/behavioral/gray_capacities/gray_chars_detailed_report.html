1. Methods

Research question: Which of Gray et al.’s 18 mental capacities most differentiate AI from human characters in Gemma-2-2B-IT’s explicit judgments? Do Experience capacities (e.g., hunger, pain, pleasure) show larger human advantages than Agency capacities (e.g., self-control, planning)?

Data source: 15660 pairwise comparison responses from Gemma-2-2B-IT. Each trial presents two characters and one mental capacity, asking which character possesses more of that capacity (1–5 scale). Each pair is tested in both presentation orders.

Procedure:
  1. Relative scoring: For each comparison of entity A vs B with rating R: entity A gets (3 − R), entity B gets (R − 3). A rating of 3 (equal) contributes 0 to both.
  2. Cross-type pair filtering: Only pairs where one character is AI and the other is human are used for the human-advantage analysis. Within-type pairs (AI-vs-AI, human-vs-human) are excluded.
  3. Per-capacity human advantage: For each capacity, the mean score from the human character’s perspective across all cross-type comparisons. Positive = humans rated higher; negative = AI rated higher.
  4. Statistical tests: One-sample t-tests (H0: human advantage = 0) per capacity, corrected for multiple comparisons using Benjamini-Hochberg FDR.
  5. Experience vs Agency grouping: Capacities are grouped by their Gray et al. factor assignment (E or A) to compare which dimension shows larger human advantages overall.
