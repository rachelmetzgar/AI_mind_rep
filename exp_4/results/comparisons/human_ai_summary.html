1. Overview

This report extends the Gray, Gray, & Wegner (2007) mind perception paradigm from the original 13 entities to 30 characters (15 AI systems + 15 human characters). The Human-AI Adaptation branch tests whether LLMs systematically differentiate AI from human characters in the mind perception space.

Key questions:

  • Do LLMs assign different mental capacity profiles to AI vs human characters?
  • Does the two-factor (Experience/Agency) structure from Gray et al. emerge with 30 characters?
  • Is AI-human separation driven by descriptions or by names alone?

3. Methods

  1. Pairwise comparisons: For each pair of characters and each of 18 mental capacities, the model judges which character is more capable. With 30 characters, this gives C(30,2) × 18 = 7,830 comparisons (fewer if some characters are excluded due to data issues).
  2. PCA with varimax rotation: Following Gray et al., we compute the correlation matrix across capacities, run PCA, retain factors with eigenvalue > 1 (minimum 2), and apply varimax rotation. Factor scores are rescaled to 0–1.
  3. AI-Human separation test: Mann-Whitney U tests compare mean factor scores for AI characters vs human characters on each factor.
  4. Names-only control (chat models): Repeats the pairwise paradigm using only character names (no descriptions), testing whether the model differentiates AI from human based on name alone.

9. Key Takeaways

  • LLaMA-2-13B Chat: 6-factor structure (eigenvalues: 8.6, 2.0, 1.6, 1.3, 1.2, 1.1). The mind perception space is more fragmented than the classic two-factor model.
  • LLaMA-2-13B Chat: Significant AI-human separation on F1 (p=0.021). The model distinguishes AI from human characters in its mind perception judgments.
  • LLaMA-2-13B Chat (names-only): No significant separation with names alone. Descriptions are necessary for the model to differentiate AI from human.
  • LLaMA-2-13B Base: Clean two-factor structure emerges (eigenvalues: 16.5, 0.5), mirroring the Experience/Agency dimensions from Gray et al.
  • LLaMA-2-13B Base: Significant AI-human separation on F2 (p=0.001). The model distinguishes AI from human characters in its mind perception judgments.
  • LLaMA-3-8B Base: Clean two-factor structure emerges (eigenvalues: 16.5, 0.7), mirroring the Experience/Agency dimensions from Gray et al.
  • LLaMA-3-8B Base: Significant AI-human separation on F2 (p=0.000). The model distinguishes AI from human characters in its mind perception judgments.
