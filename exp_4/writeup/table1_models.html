Table 1. Summary of large language models evaluated in the present study. All 11 models span three developer families (Meta, Google DeepMind, Alibaba Cloud), two generations of the LLaMA architecture, and a range of post-training alignment procedures from no alignment (base models) to multi-stage reinforcement learning pipelines. Each instruction-tuned/chat model is paired with its base pretrained counterpart to isolate alignment effects. “Parameters” refers to total (dense) parameter count. “Post-Training Alignment” describes the sequence of supervised fine-tuning (SFT), reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF), and direct preference optimization (DPO) applied after pretraining; base models received no post-training. “ToM Benchmark Performance” summarizes published results from behavioral Theory of Mind evaluations where available; most models in this set have not been evaluated on standard ToM benchmarks, underscoring the need for the mechanistic representational approach adopted here. Architectural details include hidden dimensionality, number of transformer layers, attention mechanism (MHA = multi-head attention; GQA = grouped-query attention), and vocabulary size. Training data volume is reported in tokens. All instruction-tuned/chat models use the chat-formatted variant available on HuggingFace.
